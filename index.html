About This Project

This "Parent Awareness Video Series" is a community service initiative developed by Computer Science Engineering students from Vizianagaram. Our goal is to provide accessible, easy-to-understand guidance for parents on how to ensure their children's safety and well-being in the digital world. We believe that informed parents are the first line of defense against online risks.

Through engaging, AI-generated animated videos, we aim to cover essential 'Do's and Don'ts' of internet use, helping families navigate the complexities of online interactions, privacy, and responsible digital citizenship. Your child's safety is our priority, and this project serves to bridge the knowledge gap for parents in our community.

The DO's of Online Safety

Do: Talk to Your Kids Openly

Learn how open communication builds trust and encourages children to share online concerns.

Do: Set Clear Digital Boundaries

Establish rules for screen time, content access, and online interactions together as a family.

Do: Use Parental Control Tools Wisely

Understand how to utilize software and device settings to create a safer online environment.

Do: Review Privacy Settings Regularly

Learn how to check and adjust privacy settings on apps, social media, and devices for your child's safety.

Do: Monitor Online Activity (Responsibly)

Tips for monitoring your child's online activity while respecting their growing independence.

Do: Teach Critical Thinking for Online Content

Help children evaluate information and identify misinformation or inappropriate content online.

The DON'Ts of Online Safety

Don't: Share Personal Info Online Recklessly

Understand the dangers of oversharing and how to protect your child's privacy and identity.

Don't: Engage with Strangers Online Alone

Teach your children about the risks of interacting with unknown individuals in online games or social platforms.

Don't: Ignore Warning Signs of Cyberbullying

Learn to recognize the red flags that your child might be a victim or perpetrator of cyberbullying.

Don't: Fall for Phishing Scams or Fake Links

Educate yourself and your children about identifying suspicious emails, messages, and links to avoid online fraud.

Don't: Allow Unrestricted App Downloads

Control what apps your children download and understand their permissions and content.

Don't: Share Passwords or Account Access

Emphasize the importance of strong, unique passwords and never sharing them with anyone.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is considered "personal information" online?

This includes your full name, address, phone number, school, specific age, photos that show identifiable landmarks, and even your daily routines. Teach children to be cautious about revealing any details that could help someone identify or locate them.

How much screen time is appropriate for my child?

Screen time guidelines vary by age. For younger children (under 5), limit to 1 hour of high-quality programming. For older children, focus on balanced usage that doesn't interfere with sleep, physical activity, or schoolwork. The quality of content and parental involvement are often more important than strict time limits.

What should I do if my child is cyberbullied?

Stay calm and supportive. Don't take away their devices immediately, as it might make them less likely to report. Document everything (screenshots, messages). Block the bully. Report to the platform, school authorities, and if severe or involving threats, report to local police or the National Cybercrime Reporting Portal (1930).

What are common online scams targeting parents in India?

Parents are often targeted by phishing scams (fake emails/messages asking for personal info), online job scams, lottery/prize scams, and even fake customer support calls. Always verify sender identity, check for suspicious links, and never share OTPs or banking passwords. Be wary of offers that seem too good to be true.

How can I report online fraud or cyberbullying in India?

For any cybercrime, including fraud or cyberbullying, immediately report it on the National Cybercrime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or call their helpline 1930. You can also approach your local police station's cyber cell. Preserve all evidence (screenshots, chat logs, URLs).

What is netiquette, and why is it important for my child?

Netiquette refers to the rules of polite and appropriate behavior online. It's crucial for your child to understand that online interactions have real-world consequences. Teaching them empathy, respect, and critical thinking before they post or share helps them avoid online conflicts, maintain a positive digital footprint, and stay safe from malicious actors.
